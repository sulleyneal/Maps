RUSTON

Seat of Lincoln Parish — a railroad town of 1884
in the piney hills of North Louisiana

❧

Ruston was born in 1884, when Robert E. Russ offered up his land along the newly laid Vicksburg, Shreveport & Pacific Railroad and a town — “Russ Town” — grew at the depot, soon taking the parish seat from old Vienna just to the north. The Rock Island line later crossed the V.S.&P. here, and the crossing made the town.

Today some 22,000 people live among these wooded ridges, midway between Shreveport and Monroe on Interstate 20. Louisiana Tech anchors the west side of town, Grambling State lies four miles further, and each June the streets downtown fill for the Louisiana Peach Festival, held since 1951.

      The Lay of the Land

      Ruston sits in the rolling uplands of North Louisiana — sandy, pine-covered hills quite unlike the flat delta to the east. Creeks running off these ridges feed Choudrant Bayou to the east and the headwaters of Bayou D’Arbonne to the north.
